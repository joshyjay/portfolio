About

Over three years at DTU I watched a system I built become the equipment a test centre reached for by default, replacing inspection that used to be done by hand. That shift, from a working prototype to something people genuinely rely on, is the part of technical work I care about most.

I am a chemical and process engineer who builds and operates the data and imaging systems behind technical work. My PhD measured antifouling coating performance from underwater images at the CoaST Maritime Test Centre, where I designed and deployed three autonomous camera systems that ran continuously for more than two years. Around them I built the machine learning and image processing at the core, and the databases and automated deployment that carried raw field images through to quantitative results.

Machine learning and computer vision are tools I reach for rather than the whole of what I do. What I care about is the path from a physical process to a result someone can trust. I came to it across more than one discipline and more than one country, training as an engineer in Ghana, moving into coatings and materials research in Egypt, and building applied data systems in Denmark.

I am based in Copenhagen and drawn to problems where a physical process meets the software built to measure it.
